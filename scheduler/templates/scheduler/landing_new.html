Have you ever started a project and then midway left it just because you found out some similar projects are available which are not completely similar but due to demotivation you forget your project’s USP? Or have you ever thought of having a much simpler and a light version of project management tool that is only targetted to Makers/Indepenedent Developers?

There are too many project management tools available out there, then why a new one? Ummmm, I don’t think anyone of them is yet targeted completely for independent developers or makers. I think we need a lighter tool. Currently the management tools are too heavy and does more than we want them to do. I think we needed a tool that focusses on the progress of the tool more than how the tasks are being implemented. We want a Progress Management Tool rather than a Project Management tool.

Taking a project from idea state to launch state is one hell of a task. I have worked on few side projects myself and scrapped them before launch. But since last 3 months I have been freelancing and during that time as my deadlines were tight I used to keep an excel sheet ( example below) to schedule all the tasks and also control the progress. But I think its easier when its freelancing to keep a simple spreadsheet as its not your product but when it’s your own product you need something more. Thus idea of ProgressBoard crossed my mind.

ProgressBoard - just like my spreadsheet scheduling with simple status for each task and no heavy features that anyway most of them don’t want to use or pay for. And on top of merging it with agile based point system, where each task has some point assigned and based on that the project’s progress will be tracked.

But while creating a MVP, the other main problem is motivation. Usually when we start a project we get hyped up about the idea. And we want to get to it as fast as possible. We may sometime leave the project we are currently working on to start working on this new idea. And this cycle continues. Sooner or later we realise that Oh! this idea doesn’t seem much interesting now and we get onto something next which is great.

Thus keeping a deadline timer tick tocking in front of you with a progress bar showing how much is still left to motivate you. Also while we get the idea our motivations behind that ideas are super clear which might fade in some days so saving it at the time of project creation only and use them to remind yourself whenever you forget the main motivations and get going again.
